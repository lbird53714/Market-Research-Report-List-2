<p><p>The nuclear reactor market includes various types of reactors with distinct operating mechanisms. Pressurized water reactors (PWR) and boiling water reactors (BWR) are popular designs that use water as a coolant. Pressurized heavy water reactors (PHWR) use heavy water as a coolant and a moderator. Gas-cooled reactors comprise advanced gas-cooled reactors (AGR) and magnox reactors, which use carbon dioxide gas for cooling. Fast neutron reactors (FBR) rely on fast neutrons for sustaining the chain reaction. Light water graphite reactors encompass RBMK and EGP designs, which use graphite as a moderator and water as a coolant.</p></p>
